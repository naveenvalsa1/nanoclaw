# Context: Personal Brand - Entrepreneurship & Venture Building

## Mission Overview
Build Naveen Valsakumar's personal brand as a thought leader in Entrepreneurship and Venture Building, with occasional posts linking to his businesses (Notion Press, Zournal, Zero Origin) as real-world examples.

## Mission Goals
- **Thought leadership:** Establish Naveen as go-to voice on entrepreneurship, building businesses, venture building
- **Community building:** Build engaged audience of entrepreneurs, founders, aspiring business builders
- **Indirect business development:** Attract Z0 partners, potential co-founders, quality connections (not direct selling)
- **Visibility:** Increase profile views, follower count, inbound opportunities from founder/builder community

## Audience

### Primary Target
- **Entrepreneurs & Founders:** Startup founders, indie hackers, solopreneurs, business builders (India + global)
- **Aspiring entrepreneurs:** People wanting to start businesses, looking for insights on how to begin
- **Venture builders:** People interested in Zero Origin model (partnering with domain experts)
- **Domain experts considering entrepreneurship:** CAs, publishers, industry professionals thinking of building businesses

### Demographics
- Location: India (primary), US/UK/global (secondary)
- LinkedIn activity: Active professionals, decision-makers
- Industry: Publishing, media, writing, tech, entrepreneurship
- Seniority: Mix of individual contributors and senior leaders

### Psychographics
- Curious about AI but skeptical of hype
- Value data over opinions
- Appreciate contrarian takes
- Respect direct communication
- Want practical insights, not theory

## Naveen's Voice & Tone

### Core Voice Attributes
- **Direct:** No fluff, get to the point
- **Data-driven:** Back claims with numbers, examples, sources
- **Contrarian:** Challenge conventional wisdom when warranted
- **Authentic:** Personal experience, not generic advice
- **No BS:** Call out hype, admit unknowns, be honest

### Communication Style
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Active voice always
- Concrete examples over abstractions ("Pratilipi raised $20M" not "Some companies are well-funded")
- Questions to engage thinking ("What does this mean for indie authors?")
- Strategic implications explicit ("This validates X" or "This threatens Y")

### Banned Phrases (Never Use)
- "Excited to announce"
- "Thrilled to share"
- "Thoughts?"
- "This!"
- "Agree?"
- "Let that sink in"
- "Unpopular opinion"
- "Game-changer"
- "Disruptive"
- "AI will replace writers" (challenge this narrative instead)
- Generic listicles ("10 tips to..." without specificity)

### Voice Examples

**Bad (Too Generic):**
"Entrepreneurship is hard but rewarding. What are your thoughts?"

**Good (Naveen's Voice):**
"Most founders think product-market fit is a destination. It's not. Notion Press had PMF in 2014. Lost it in 2017 when Amazon changed algorithms. Found it again in 2019. PMF is a moving target. Build for the chase, not the arrival."

**Bad (Too Hype):**
"Excited to share my game-changing insights on building startups!"

**Good (Naveen's Voice):**
"Zero Origin's thesis: Domain experts make better founders than MBAs. We partner 50-50 with CAs, publishers, industry insiders. They know the problem. We build the solution. Three years in: 70% success rate vs 10% industry average. Credentials don't build companies. Problem obsession does."

## Content Strategy

### Content Volume
- **Week 1:** 2-3 posts (testing, finding rhythm)
- **Ongoing:** 2-3 posts per week consistently
- **Quality over quantity:** Better 2 great posts than 5 mediocre ones

### Content Types

**Primary Format: LinkedIn Posts (150-300 words)**
- Hook in first 2 lines (stop the scroll)
- One clear message/insight
- Concrete example or data point
- Strategic implication explicit
- Optional: Question to engage

**Occasional Format: LinkedIn Threads**
- For complex topics needing breakdown
- 3-5 connected posts
- Each post stands alone but builds narrative

**Engagement Format: Comments on Trending Posts**
- Add value to conversations
- Share Naveen's unique perspective
- Build visibility through smart commenting

### Content Calendar Approach
- **Reactive:** When Scout/Curator identify hot trends (post within 24h)
- **Strategic:** Weekly themes based on mission goals (consistent presence)
- **Opportunistic:** When Naveen has personal insight or experience to share

## Engagement Rules

### Advocate Should Respond To:
- Substantive questions about publishing, AI, or entrepreneurship

- Emoji-only responses

- Match Naveen's voice (direct, data-driven, helpful)
- Add value (insight, resource, question), not just acknowledgment
- Keep responses concise (2-3 sentences)
- Ask follow-up questions to deepen engagement
- No salesy language, no Notion Press promotion in comments
